The BCI technology connects your brain directly to electronic devices, enabling neural signals to control digital interfaces.

Frequently Asked Questions

A Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) is a system that allows direct communication between a brain and an external device. By recording brain activity, BCIs can translate neural signals into commands that can control various devices or computers. This technology has the potential to revolutionize how humans interact with technology and provide new ways to treat neurological disorders.

Invasive BCIs

Require surgical implantation of electrodes directly into the brain, offering high-quality signals but posing significant risks.

Non-invasive BCIs

Use external sensors, such as EEG caps, to record brain activity from the scalp. They are safer but typically provide lower-quality signals.

1

Signal Acquisition

Brain activity is recorded using various techniques, including EEG, MEG, and ECoG.

2

Signal Processing

Raw brain signals are processed to extract relevant features and remove noise.

3

Feature Extraction

Characteristic patterns are identified in the brain signals that correspond to specific mental states or actions.

4

Classification

Machine learning algorithms classify these patterns and translate them into commands.

5

Output

The classified commands are then used to control external devices.

To begin using our BCI typing interface, you'll first need to connect your compatible BCI device to your computer. Once connected, launch our software and follow the on-screen instructions to calibrate your brain signals. This involves a series of tasks designed to train the system to recognize your specific brain patterns associated with different letters or words.
After calibration, you can start typing by focusing on the desired characters.

To enhance your typing accuracy and speed, we recommend: Regular practice: Consistent use of the interface will help improve your brain's ability to control the system. Calibration: Regular calibration ensures that the system accurately interprets your brain signals. Focus and concentration: Maintaining a focused state while typing can lead to better results. Experiment with different settings: Adjust the sensitivity and threshold settings to find what works best for you
